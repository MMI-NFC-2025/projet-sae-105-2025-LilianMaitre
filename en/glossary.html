Glossary

This section provides clear and accessible definitions of the key terms used in our articles about women in science, astronomy and science communication.

It is designed to help readers better understand the concepts, phenomena and sometimes technical words encountered when discovering Vera Rubin’s work and that of other great pioneers.

Astrophysicist
A scientist specialising in the study of stars, galaxies and phenomena in the Universe. Feminine form of astrophysicist.
Dark matter
A form of invisible matter that emits neither light nor energy but exerts a gravitational pull. It accounts for most of the mass in the Universe.
Cosmology
The science that studies the origin, structure and evolution of the Universe as a whole.
Matilda effect
A phenomenon in which discoveries made by women are downplayed, forgotten or attributed to their male colleagues.
Galactic rotation
The movement of stars and gas revolving around the centre of a galaxy.
Popularisation
The act of explaining science in a clear and accessible way to a non‑specialist audience.
Cosmic expansion
The fact that the Universe is constantly expanding, with galaxies moving further away from each other over time.
