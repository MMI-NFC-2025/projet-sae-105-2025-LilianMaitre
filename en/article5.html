“A Brief History of
Matter and the Universe”

An accessible and poetic work, Hubert Reeves’ “A Brief History of Matter and the Universe” introduces readers of all ages to the mysteries of the cosmos, mixing science, philosophy and wonder to better understand our place in the Universe.

A book to understand the history of the cosmos

“A Brief History of Matter and the Universe”, written by Hubert Reeves and his colleagues, is a key book for introducing the history and mysteries of the cosmos. [web:7]

The authors trace the birth of the Universe, the formation of the first elements, cosmic expansion, the role of galaxies and stars, and the enigma of dark matter using clear, vivid and often poetic language. [web:16][web:11]

The book raises essential questions: what is matter, how are the infinitely small and infinitely large connected, how did Earth and life emerge from billions of years of cosmic evolution, and what is the nature of the invisible matter that makes up most of the Universe. [web:16][web:11]

Between science and philosophy, what place is there for humans?

Beyond scientific explanations, the book invites readers to reflect on humanity’s place in the cosmos, linking scientific knowledge with a sense of wonder and philosophical questions about meaning. [web:7][web:10]

Designed for curious minds of all ages, it relies on illustrations, anecdotes, interviews and Hubert Reeves’ talent as a populariser of science to make complex ideas both understandable and moving. [web:18][web:21]
