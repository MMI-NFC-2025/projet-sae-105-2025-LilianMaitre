The gaze that changed science

Introduction

At a time when gender equality and access to knowledge are major issues, this website offers a space for information, reflection and discovery.

It invites visitors to explore or rediscover overlooked women scientists, to question how their contributions have been erased using Vera Rubin’s story as a guiding thread, and to celebrate the diversity of women’s careers in science.

Vera Rubin

Born in 1928, Vera Rubin faced discrimination to become a pioneering astrophysicist. Her work on galaxy rotation curves revealed dark matter and revolutionised cosmology, while her career illustrates the Matilda effect.
