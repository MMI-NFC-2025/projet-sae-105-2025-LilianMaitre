Interstellar is not the only film that fights for women in the field of astronomy. Here are several other works that follow this pattern:

Les figures de l’ombre

Allociné page

The extraordinary story of three African-American scientists who helped the United States take the lead in the space race by putting astronaut John Glenn into orbit. Kept in the shadows by their male colleagues and in a country plagued by deep inequalities, their long-unknown story is finally being brought to the screen.

Étoile du Nord

Teaching sheet

This multi-episode documentary series follows astronomer Laurie Rousseau-Nepton, who leads a research project at the Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope. She combines sky observation, Innu culture, intergenerational transmission, and popularization, offering an original perspective on astronomy and the role of Indigenous women scientists.

Gravity

Allociné page

The film follows Dr. Ryan Stone, an expert in medical engineering, on her first space mission. After the shuttle is destroyed, she finds herself isolated in space and must survive alone. Beyond the visual spectacle, the story centers on a female scientist confronted with her fears, loss, and resilience. The film is directed by James Cameron, known for his work on Titanic and Avatar.

Proxima

Allociné page

The feature film follows Sarah, a French astronaut who is preparing for a year-long space mission while managing her relationship with her eight-year-old daughter. The film addresses mental load, the balance between scientific vocation and family life, and the place of women in space programs.

Chercheuses d’étoiles

Watch the video

The documentary series “Star Seekers” highlights the major astronomical discoveries made by women who have long been forgotten. Between exploring dark matter, observing the deep sky, and the history of science, it extends the themes of the Étoiles oubliées website.
