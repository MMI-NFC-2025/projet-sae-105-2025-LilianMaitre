Gallery of films/series related to astronomy and the emancipation of women

Interstellar is not the only film that gives a voice to women in astronomy. This page presents a selection of works that combine space, science and women’s stories, to watch, analyse and discuss.

The figures in the shadows

The extraordinary story of three African‑American scientists who helped the United States take the lead in the space race by putting astronaut John Glenn into orbit, after having long been kept in the shadows by their male colleagues and by a deeply unequal society.

View the film details

North Star

Five short episodes follow Laurie Rousseau‑Nepton, who leads a research project at the Canada‑France‑Hawaii Telescope. The series links her Innu culture, her observational skills and her work on star formation, offering a powerful, accessible way to teach astronomy.

Discover the series

Gravity

During her first mission, Dr Ryan Stone, a medical engineer, finds herself stranded in space with veteran astronaut Matt Kowalsky after a catastrophe destroys their shuttle. Cut off from Earth, she must fight panic and push her limits to try to return home.

View the film details

Proxima

Sarah, a French astronaut, prepares for a year‑long mission in space. As the only woman in the crew, she must face demanding training and, above all, the emotional challenge of leaving her eight‑year‑old daughter behind.

View the film details

Chercheuses d’étoiles

This documentary tells the story of great astronomical discoveries made by women whose names have often been forgotten by history, while exploring dark matter and the origins of the Universe.

Watch the documentary
